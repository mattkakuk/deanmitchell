## **Why Fire Damage Is More Than What You Can See**

### **Smoke and Soot Travel Everywhere**

Even if flames stayed in one room, smoke spreads like an invisible storm
cloud. It seeps into insulation, HVAC pathways, cabinets, and fabrics. Soot
can also be corrosive, meaning it doesn’t just “sit there”—it actively damages
surfaces over time.

### **Water Damage Often Follows Fire**

Fire suppression creates its own emergency: soaked drywall, drenched flooring,
and trapped moisture behind walls. Without fast drying, secondary damage can
multiply.

## **Fire Recovery Tips While You Wait for Professionals**

### **Do Not Disturb Soot-Covered Surfaces**

Wiping incorrectly can grind soot deeper into materials and cause permanent
staining.

### **Avoid Using Electronics Until Cleared**

Smoke residue can damage internal components, and moisture increases the risk.

### **Ventilation Helps, But Only If Safe**

If your home is cleared for entry, limited ventilation can help reduce
odor—just avoid stirring ash and soot.
