## **Understanding the “Three Types” of Fire Damage**

### **Fire Damage**

This is the obvious stuff—burnt framing, damaged drywall, charred surfaces,
compromised structure. It’s what you can see at a glance.

### **Smoke Damage**

Smoke travels further than flames and can damage paint, insulation, furniture,
electronics, and HVAC systems. Even a small kitchen fire can spread smoke
residue across an entire home.

### **Soot Damage**

Soot is tiny, acidic, and stubborn. It stains, corrodes, and embeds itself
into porous materials. Left untreated, it can permanently damage surfaces and
keep odor trapped indoors.

## **Common Signs You Need Professional Fire Restoration (Even After a Small
Fire)**

### **The Smell Won’t Go Away**

If the odor lingers after “cleaning,” it means smoke particles are still
embedded—often inside porous surfaces or hidden cavities.

### **Soot Keeps Reappearing**

Wiping soot without proper filtration can smear it and spread it. If black
residue returns, you likely need professional soot removal and air scrubbing.

### **Walls Look Yellow or Shadowy**

Smoke staining doesn’t always show up immediately. Over time, it can discolor
paint and bleed through coatings if not treated correctly.

### **You Notice Irritation or Breathing Discomfort Indoors**

Smoke residue and airborne particulates can aggravate allergies and
respiratory issues. Air quality is a big deal after a fire.

## **The Real Risk After a Fire: Secondary Damage**

### **Water Damage from Fire Suppression**

Even if the fire was contained quickly, water can soak floors, baseboards, and
subflooring. Left alone, that moisture becomes a structural problem.

### **Mold Growth**

In Florida, moisture plus time equals mold. Fire restoration often includes
drying strategies to prevent microbial growth from becoming the next headache.

### **Corrosion and Etching**

Soot is acidic. It can corrode metal, pit glass, and damage finishes if it
sits too long. The sooner the cleanup begins, the more we can preserve.

## **Fire Restoration Tips for Homeowners (What to Do Right Now)**

### **Do Not Start Scrubbing Soot**

It seems helpful, but soot can smear and embed deeper. Improper cleaning can
make restoration harder and more expensive.

### **Avoid Running the HVAC**

If smoke residue is present, running the HVAC can distribute particulates
through the duct system.

### **Secure the Property**

If windows or doors are compromised, secure the structure to prevent theft,
weather intrusion, or further loss.

### **Most Important: Call a Restoration Team Early**

Early action often saves materials—and money. It’s like catching a leak before
it becomes a flood.
