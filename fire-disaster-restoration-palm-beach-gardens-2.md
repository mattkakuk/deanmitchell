## **What “Fire Disaster Restoration” Actually Includes**

### **The Three Types of Fire Damage We Usually See**

Fire loss typically breaks into three categories:

  * **Structural damage** (framing, drywall, roofing, supports)
  * **Smoke and soot damage** (walls, ceilings, ducts, contents)
  * **Water damage** (from sprinklers or firefighting efforts)

Even if the fire looks “contained,” smoke travels like gossip—fast, far, and
into places you wouldn’t expect.

### **Why Smoke Damage Can Be Worse Than Burn Damage**

Smoke particles are tiny, acidic, and stubborn. They can:

  * Corrode metal
  * Discolor walls and fixtures
  * Embed odor into porous surfaces (wood, fabric, insulation)

That’s why professional **Fire Disaster Restoration** isn’t optional—it’s the
difference between “looks okay” and “actually safe and clean.”

## **First 24 Hours After a Fire: What I Recommend You Do**

### **1) Prioritize Safety Over Salvage**

Before you grab anything, make sure the property has been cleared as safe by
the fire department or a qualified professional. Hidden hazards are common:
weakened floors, electrical damage, and sharp debris.

### **2) Don’t Start Scrubbing Soot**

This surprises people. Soot smears easily and can permanently stain. If you
wipe the wrong way with the wrong cleaner, you can lock the damage in.

### **3) Call a Restoration Team Early**

The sooner we start, the more we can save. Odors set in, corrosion begins, and
water damage escalates quickly. Early action reduces total restoration time
and cost.

## **Our Fire Disaster Restoration Process in Palm Beach Gardens**

### **Step 1: Emergency Assessment & Damage Mapping**

I begin with a full walkthrough. We identify:

  * Fire origin and severity patterns
  * Smoke travels paths
  * Soot type (wet soot, dry soot, protein residue, etc.)
  * Water intrusion zones

This matters because each soot type behaves differently—kind of like different
kinds of sand. Some brushes off. Someclings like glue.

### **Step 2: Board-Up, Roof Tarping, and Site Security**

If windows are blown out or the roof is compromised, we stabilize immediately.
Securing the site prevents:

  * Additional weather damage
  * Unauthorized entry
  * Insurance complications

### **Step 3: Smoke, Soot, and Odor Removal**

Here’s where the real craft comes in. We use specialized methods such as:

  * HEPA air scrubbers
  * Negative air setups
  * Surface-specific soot removal
  * Odor neutralization (not “cover-ups”)

When done right, your home shouldn’t smell like fire ever again.

### **Step 4: Water Extraction and Drying**

If firefighting water is present, we shift into[ **Water Damage
Mitigation**](https://www.google.com/maps?cid=9407597375226841223) mode—fast.

humidity.

### **Step 5: Cleaning, Sanitizing, and Decontamination**

We clean salvageable contents and structural materials using the right
chemistry for each surface. Fire residue can contain irritants and

### **Step 6: Repairs, Reconstruction, and Final Detailing**

When needed, we rebuild and restore. My goal is simple: make your property
